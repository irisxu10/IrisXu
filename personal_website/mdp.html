My Journey
in MDP

Media Design Practices (MDP) at ArtCenter is an interdisciplinary graduate program that allows me to explore the intersections of art, design, technology, research, and social impact.

Coming from a studio art background, I initially felt unsure. But soon, I realized that MDP is not only challenging — it’s also deeply inclusive. I brought in my own lens — thinking as someone who paints, observes, and senses.

Over the past year, I’ve made projects that visualize coffee memories, contributed to the design of two apps, explored alternative typography systems, created an interactive Getty card game, experimented with multi-modal information expression, and expanded my understanding of technology through making.

These works reflect my ongoing interest in crossing mediums, engaging tools critically, and expressing emotion through form.

I’ve also written two research-based papers. You can read them on my Writing Page →

Thank you for visiting. I hope you enjoy scrolling through the journey!

What's In Your Coffee Cup？

Visualizing the emotion, flavor, and memory of each cup.

This project aims to transform everyday coffee experiences into visible and shareable data expressions. Using P5.js and HTML, I developed an interactive web tool that allows users to input details of their coffee moment—such as volume, flavor direction, drinking time, and mood—and generate a unique visual pattern for each cup.

These visuals were later translated into physical forms by printing them on transparent sticker paper and mounting them onto acrylic sheets. Each acrylic piece was paired with a hand-splattered coffee canvas, creating a layered and textured record of both data and feeling.

This project is not just about taste—it’s about memory, emotion, and habit. Each coffee moment is no longer just consumed, but seen, saved, and shared.
